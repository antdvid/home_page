MY LATEST RESEARCH


Except for huge computational cost, direct numerical simulation (DNS) is a powerful and reliable approach for studying turbulent flow. Here we use DNS to study cloud entrainment and mixing processes in turbulent environment. Cloud is modelled with large amounts of simple particles, thus evaporation, condensation and inertial motion can be resolved in the simulation(4.18.2013)

Unlike the experimental methods, numerical simulation of parachute inflation and falling is economical and safe. This complex process includes the models of canopies, turbulence flow and fluid-structure interactions. We used enhanced mass-spring model to simulate the parachute canopy and fluid is solved with fractional step method and coupled with k-eps turbulence model for high Reynolds number air flow(4.18.2013)

The instability of an interface between two fluids with different densities is often called Rayleigh–Taylor instability, which occurs when the light fluid is pushing the heavier fluid. The simulation of this physical process requires preserving the discontinuity at the interface, thus a numerical method with interface tracking capability is desirable.(4.18.2013)

When a bullet travels at supersonic speed that is faster than sound speed, the air is greatly compressed at the front-most tip of the bullet, then a bullet bow shock-wave will be created and heard by any witness. In our simulation, the air flow is modelled by Euler equation and the bullet is driven by pressure difference around it. (4.18.2013)
